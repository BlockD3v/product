# Hyperliquid out-earned Ethereum. Now what?

## Angle

Hyperliquid generated $1.3M in 24h fees, beating both TRON and Ethereum. The interesting story isn't the number — it's the implication: a single-app L1 is now the fee-revenue benchmark for general-purpose smart contract platforms. That breaks the "monolithic L1" valuation framework that's dominated since 2020.

## Why now

- Recent 24h fee leaderboard shows HL > ETH > TRON
- ~13% of all global on-chain perp volume now routes through HL
- 30d perp volume >$180B (April 2026)
- Most outlets reported the headline; nobody priced the *implication*

## Key evidence

- 97% of fees → buyback/burn → ~333k HYPE/month (~$9M) burned
- April revenue $52.22M (down 14.95% MoM but still industry-leading)
- Open interest network-wide >$8B
- Compare: ETH revenue is split across thousands of dApps; HL captures it all

## Frame the piece

- Section 1: the number, in context
- Section 2: why this breaks "general-purpose vs app-chain" debate
- Section 3: what HYPE accrual looks like at scale (DCF the burn)
