# Is Hyperliquid going to $0? — the serious red-team piece

## Angle

The Motley Fool already published the question. Their answer was shallow. Write the *real* bear case — the one Hyperliquid believers should worry about — by someone who actually understands the protocol. This is a citation-magnet because nobody serious has written it.

## Why now

- Motley Fool framing is in the wild and unanswered
- Token unlock + regulatory headwinds create natural bear narrative
- Crypto-native media skew bullish — counter-position wins attention

## Bear case to develop

1. **Insider concentration** — token distribution, unlock schedule, contributor power
2. **Validator centralization** — 21 validators, geographic clustering, who runs them
3. **Polymarket / Kalshi resilience** — they have liquidity moats, regulated paths, brand
4. **Regulatory tail risk** — CFTC could go the other way; HPC win is not guaranteed
5. **Single-app dependency** — if perps narrative cools, HL has no diversification (vs ETH)
6. **Buyback dependency** — burn math only works while fees stay high; what happens in a perps drought?
7. **HIP-4 execution risk** — testnet ≠ mainnet at scale

## Tone

Not doomer. Not contrarian for its own sake. Steelman every bear point such that a HYPE bull would say "fair, that's the real risk." Then optionally rebut.
