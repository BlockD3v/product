# $408M HYPE unlocks May 6 — and core contributors get all of it

## Angle

9.92M HYPE (1% of total supply) unlocks for core contributors on May 6. The question isn't whether it dumps — it's: do contributors *sell, stake, or deploy into HIP-3 vaults*? Each path has very different second-order effects. Write a pre-event take + a retro after the unlock clears.

## Why now

- Unlock event hits May 6, 2026 (3 days from snapshot)
- Whale OI on platform: $3.9B; long/short ratio ~1.04 (basically neutral)
- Coverage so far is generic FUD listicle territory — measured take wins

## Key evidence

- 9.92M HYPE = ~$408M at current price
- Recipient: core contributors only (not investors, not foundation)
- Historical post-unlock vol: medium 7-day vol; not auto-bearish
- Buy-side absorption capacity: $1.3M/day fees + buyback program absorbs supply

## Three scenarios to write to

1. **Sell** → bearish 1–2 weeks, recovery as buyback persists
2. **Stake** → unlock is a non-event for float; bullish for security
3. **Deploy into HIP-3** → contributors become builder-deployers, $408M becomes deployer stake (covers ~20 new perp DEXs at 500k HYPE each)

### Draft v1 — pre-event take

**Headline:** $407M of HYPE unlocks for core contributors on May 6. The dump is already priced. The interesting question is what the other 95% does next.

**Lede.** On Wednesday, 9.92M HYPE — 1% of total supply, 4.16% of float, $407M at today's price — vests for core contributors. Every crypto outlet has filed the same listicle: "biggest May unlock," "watch for downside," "Arthur Hayes still bullish." Skip those. The number that actually matters isn't 9.92M — it's 422k. That's how much HYPE has been *pre-unstaked* in the days leading up to the cliff, per HyperliquidNews's tracking. Everything else is still bonded.

**Why 422k is the only signal worth watching.** Unstaking on Hyperliquid takes 7 days. Anyone planning to sell into the cliff had to start the clock by the end of April. So the wallets we can see right now — 422k HYPE, ~$17M, ~4.25% of the unlock — are the *upper bound* of what hits CEX order books in the first week. The other 9.5M sits in staking contracts or contributor wallets that haven't moved. That doesn't mean it never sells. It means the often-cited "$407M cliff" reads more like "$17M known liquid + $390M optional."

**The buy side, sized.** Hyperliquid's fee→buyback flow is $1.87M/day on a 30-day mean (DefiLlama, $56.2M over 30d), 100% routed to HYPE bids. At $41.83, that's ~45k HYPE/day of structural demand. Net of staking emissions (~27k/day), that's –18k HYPE/day of float, ~6.4M HYPE/year removed.

Now run the unlock against that:

| Sell-through | HYPE/day sold over 30d | Buyback/day | Net pressure |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5% (only the pre-unstaked 422k) | ~17k | 45k | **Net +28k/day buy** |
| 25% | ~83k | 45k | -38k/day, 4 weeks |
| 50% | ~165k | 45k | -120k/day, 6+ weeks |
| 100% | ~331k | 45k | -286k/day, 6 months to digest |

Read the on-chain pre-unstake number as the market's vote. As of today, it's voting for case 1.

**Three scenarios for what the locked 95% does.**

1. **Sell.** The base-rate take. Bearish 1–2 weeks if more than ~25% hits exchanges. Buyback flow recovers float in 30–45 days. Note: official Hyperliquid docs do not publish the contributor vesting schedule, so whether May 6 is a one-off cliff or the largest of a recurring monthly series is an inference, not a confirmed fact. Tokenomist labels it the "next unlock" but doesn't enumerate the cadence beyond. Falsifiable check: net HYPE flow onto Binance/Bybit/OKX in the 7 days after May 6.

2. **Stake.** Best case for security and worst case for FUD merchants. HYPE staked = HYPE that earns rewards + counts toward validator security + can't be sold without a 7-day unbond. If contributors stake 70%+, the unlock is a non-event for float and a positive for validator decentralization. Falsifiable check: total staked HYPE delta on May 6–13 (visible on-chain).

3. **Deploy into HIP-3.** This is the path nobody's writing about and the one with the largest second-order effect. HIP-3 lets anyone post 500k HYPE as deployer stake to spin up a permissioned perp DEX. 9.92M HYPE = 19.84 new deployer slots. If even 5% of the unlock — ~500k HYPE — goes this route, that's *one new HIP-3 perp DEX seeded directly off the unlock*. Core contributors becoming the first builders is the version of the unlock that compounds for years. Falsifiable check: new HIP-3 deployer registrations in May–June 2026.

**What I'm watching, in order of signal strength.**

1. CEX inflow on the unlock TX. If <1M HYPE moves to exchange wallets within 24 hours, the "dump" thesis is dead.
2. Net staked HYPE delta over 7 days. A flat or rising number = this was a phantom unlock.
3. New HIP-3 deployers in 60 days. The slow signal but the most consequential.
4. Buyback rate. If volume holds, the absorption math holds. If volume craters under post-unlock chop, even modest sell-through gets noisier than expected.

**The contrarian read.** "Unlock = dump" is priced. Long/short ratio sits at ~1.04 — neutral, not skewed bearish. Whale OI is $3.9B and stable. The setup is actually a positive-skew event: if contributors stake or deploy, the supply overhang vanishes and a feared catalyst becomes a non-event the market re-rates through. The asymmetry is on the upside, not the downside, because the downside is already in the chart.

**Pre-commit predictions for the retro post.**

- ≤30% of the unlock hits CEX wallets in 7 days. (P=0.65)
- Net staked HYPE *increases* by May 13 vs May 5 baseline. (P=0.55)
- ≥1 new HIP-3 deployer announces by June 6, traceable to a contributor wallet. (P=0.40)
- HYPE prints a higher low than today's $41.05 within 14 days post-unlock. (P=0.55)

Track these in the retro on May 13 and again on June 6.
