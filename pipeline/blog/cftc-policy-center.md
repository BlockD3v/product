# Hyperliquid filed with the CFTC — DeFi finally has a DC playbook

## Angle

Hyperliquid Policy Center (HPC), led by Jake Chervinsky, filed comments on the CFTC's prediction-markets ANPRM April 30. They're asking for *function-based* rules and an explicit US access path for decentralized markets. This is the regulatory-arbitrage story of 2026 — and the timing collides head-on with Democrats calling for a Kalshi/Polymarket clampdown.

## Why now

- HPC submission landed on the April 30 comment deadline
- CNBC reported Dems pushing CFTC to rein in prediction markets the same day
- CFTC simultaneously suing AZ/CT/IL to assert exclusive jurisdiction
- Almost no outlet has framed this as "DeFi got a lobbying arm"

## Key evidence

- HPC asks: function-based rules, not operator/intermediary mandates
- Explicit ask for US access path for decentralized prediction markets
- Argues decentralization improves transparency, settlement security, surveillance
- Chervinsky leadership = serious policy weight, not crypto-Twitter posturing
