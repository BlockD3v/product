# Twitter Thread Draft

---

Hyperliquid does $8.3B in daily volume. On-chain. No custody risk. Verifiable settlement.

And then you open a third-party client to trade on it — and you've just trusted a black box you can't inspect.

That's the problem we're solving with HypeTerminal.

---

Hyperliquid's entire thesis is "don't trust, verify."

The chain is open. Settlement is verifiable. Your keys, your coins.

But the moment you use a closed-source trading client, you've introduced a trust assumption at the interface layer. You're trusting it routes your orders honestly. That the risk numbers are real. That it's not doing something with your data you didn't agree to.

Open source removes that assumption. Same philosophy, extended one layer up.

---

This matters more as trading becomes autonomous.

60-80% of crypto volume is already AI-driven. The direction is clear — agents executing trades on your behalf, managing positions, running strategies while you sleep.

When an agent is doing that through a closed interface, you have no idea what's sitting between you and the chain.

You should be able to read that code.

---

The other thing open source enables: forks.

Hyperliquid is permissionless infrastructure at the execution layer. Anyone can build on it.

HypeTerminal is the same thing at the interface layer. Fork it, strip it down, rebuild it for your specific use case — a mobile-only client, a bot-first terminal, a quant desk's internal tool.

You don't start from scratch. You start from a working Hyperliquid client.

---

And because of builder codes, the forks aren't charity.

Hyperliquid pays frontends 0.1% on perps and 1% on spot for routing volume. Every serious fork that generates volume gets paid by the protocol.

You're not just giving people free code. You're giving them a business model.

---

So the structure ends up being:

Hyperliquid → permissionless execution layer  
HypeTerminal → permissionless interface layer

Same bet, one level up.

The interface shouldn't be a walled garden just because the exchange isn't.
